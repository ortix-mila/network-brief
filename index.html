⚖️ The Lawyer's Take

Think of it this way: the OSI Model is like the Model Rules of Professional Conduct — a clean, theoretical framework that separates every concern into its own chapter. It's perfect for studying and understanding the principles.

The TCP/IP Model is how law is actually practiced. In the real world, a partner doesn't carefully separate "session management" from "presentation formatting" — they just dictate a memo, hit send, and it gets there. TCP/IP merges the theoretical layers into practical ones that real protocols use.

Why learn both? When something goes wrong — like an intercepted communication or a data breach — you use the OSI model to diagnose which layer was compromised, the same way you'd trace which step in a legal process was violated. "Was the breach at the application level (someone hacked the web portal)? Or at the physical level (someone tapped a cable)?"
